Introduction

Solid waste management is essential for maintaining environmental health and sustainability. This project explores the issues of solid waste management and examines solutions implemented by our group at Lovely Professional University (LPU) and similar external initiatives.

Literature Review

Background on Solid Waste Management

Solid waste management involves collecting, transporting, and processing waste materials. Common challenges include lack of infrastructure, awareness, and regulatory enforcement. Recent advances emphasize not just disposal but recycling and waste minimization.

Technological Approaches

Key technologies include IoT-enabled smart bins for monitoring waste levels, predictive analytics using machine learning, and automated recycling systems. These advancements improve efficiency and reduce environmental impact.

Case Studies

Examples include cities like San Francisco and Tokyo, where data-driven and community-supported waste management strategies have shown positive outcomes.

Evaluation of Findings

Our initiatives at LPU led to optimized waste collection through smart bin systems, resulting in reduced fuel usage and costs. Community engagement also boosted recycling rates on campus.
